How the Internet Works


According to Simple English Wiki, the Internet is the biggest world-wide communication network of computers. The Internet has millions of smaller domestic, academic, business, and government networks, which together carry many different kinds of information. The short form of internet is the 'net'. The World Wide Web is one of its biggest services. It is used by billions of people all over the world. Logo of DARPA, an organization funded by the US government, which helped develop the Internet The Internet was developed in the United States by the "United States Department of Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency" (DARPA). The Internet was first connected in October of 1969 and was called ARPANET. The World Wide Web was created at CERN in Switzerland in 1990 by a British (UK) scientist named Tim Berners-Lee. Today, people can pay money to access the Internet from internet service providers. Some services on the Internet cost nothing to use. Sometimes people who offer these free services use advertising to make money. Censorship and freedom of speech on the Internet can be controversial.







5 Components of a Computer


ISP

    Stands for Internet Service Provider
    ISPs are companies that provide internet service to customers for a fee
    Some examples of ISPs are: Xfinity, CenturyLink, AT&T, etc.



IP Address

    Stands for Internet Protocol Address
    It is a unique numerical address that identifies a device or a local network.
    IP addresses provide a way for the internet to differentiate between computers, routers, and websites



ALU

    Stands for Arithmetic Logic Unit
    An arithmetic-logic unit (ALU) is the part of a computer processor (CPU) that carries out arithmetic and logic operations on the operands in computer instruction words
    Mathematician John von Neumann proposed the ALU concept in 1945 in a report on the foundations for a new computer called the EDVAC



Control Unit

    The control unit (CU) is a component of a computer's central processing unit (CPU) that directs the operation of the processor.
    It is the "brain within the brain", as it controls what happens inside the processor, which in turn controls the rest of the compute



CPU

    Stands for Central Processing Unit
    is the electronic circuitry that executes instructions comprising a computer program.
    The CPU performs basic arithmetic, logic, controlling, and input/output operations specified by the instructions in the program
